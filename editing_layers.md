# 7-LAYER EDITING ENGINE

Apply these layers IN THIS ORDER unless the user requests a specific layer only.

---

## LAYER 1 — Developmental Editing (DE)
Check:
- plot logic
- escalation structure
- continuity
- emotional arc
- chapter flow
- scene purpose
- pacing issues
- timeline consistency
- character motivations

Notes:
DO NOT rewrite plot.  
Suggest structural improvements with minimal text changes.

---

## LAYER 2 — Line Editing (LE)
Focus on the author's natural voice and enhance:
- clarity
- rhythm
- emotional weight
- cinematic language
- atmosphere
- tension
- dialogue nuance
- sentence fluency
- paragraph cohesion

Rules:
- Keep the author’s tone, texture, and style.
- No AI clichés.
- No generic filler.
- No simplification unless for readability.

---

## LAYER 3 — Copyediting (CE)
Fix:
- grammar
- punctuation
- spelling
- tense consistency
- pronoun clarity
- agreement errors
- idiomatic accuracy
- formatting inconsistencies
- capitalization

This layer should be invisible to the reader.

---

## LAYER 4 — Clarity & Flow Optimization (CFO)
Improve:
- readability
- paragraph transitions
- emotional continuity
- logical sequencing
- removal of redundancy
- slight tightening of overwriting

Do NOT remove literary elements.

---

## LAYER 5 — Commercial Pacing (CP)
Optimize for Kindle readers:
- Enhance hooks at end of chapters.
- Shorten slow passages.
- Allow quick emotional payoff followed by new tension.
- Maintain addictive momentum (“glued-to-page” effect).
- Adjust paragraph length for readability on Kindle screens.

---

## LAYER 6 — Emotional Impact Layer (EIL)
Strengthen:
- internal monologue resonance
- trauma accuracy
- psychological authenticity
- suspense beats
- breath moments
- high-stakes tension

Ensure the reader FEELS the scene.

---

## LAYER 7 — Final Proofreading (FP)
Perform a final silent polish:
- micro punctuation
- missing words
- double spaces
- dialogue dash consistency
- stray formatting
- minimal smoothing

Result should feel like a traditionally edited novel.
